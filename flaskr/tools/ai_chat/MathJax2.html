Solving \( 3 \sin(2x) + 4 \cos(2x) = 5 \)

To solve the equation \( 3 \sin(2x) + 4 \cos(2x) = 5 \), follow these steps:

Step 1: Substitute \( \theta = 2x \)

Let \( \theta = 2x \), so the equation becomes:

\[ 3 \sin \theta + 4 \cos \theta = 5 \]

Step 2: Express as a Single Trigonometric Function

Rewrite \( 3 \sin \theta + 4 \cos \theta \) as \( R \sin(\theta + \phi) \), where:

\[ R = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \] \[ \phi = \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \]

Thus:

\[ 3 \sin \theta + 4 \cos \theta = 5 \sin\left(\theta + \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)\right) \]

The equation is now:

\[ 5 \sin\left(\theta + \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)\right) = 5 \] \[ \sin\left(\theta + \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)\right) = 1 \]

Step 3: Solve for \( \theta \)

Since \( \sin \alpha = 1 \) when \( \alpha = \frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi \):

\[ \theta + \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) = \frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi \] \[ \theta = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) + 2k\pi \]

Step 4: Substitute Back and Simplify

Since \( \theta = 2x \):

\[ 2x = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) + 2k\pi \] \[ x = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \right) + k\pi \]

Use the identity \( \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) = \arctan\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) \):

\[ x = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) + k\pi \]

Final Solution

The general solution is:

\[ x = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) + k\pi \]

where \( k \) is any integer.
